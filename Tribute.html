OHOTUOWO OGBECHE

Barrister-at-Law

Miss Ohotuowo Ogbeche is a lawyer per excellence, graduating from the prestigious University of Ilorin, Kwara state, in the top five per cent of her class with a first class degree. Afterwards, she completed requirements for her Law Degree at the Nigerian Law School, Kaduna Campus, where she also graduated with a First Class again. After practicing Law for a year as an associate with a prestigious law firm in Lagos that retained her after her National Youth Service, she got a job with a Human Rights Organisation to advocate for the rights of women and children, reaffirming her status as a feminist. Currently, she is studying to get her Masters in Law at the prestigious University of Pretoria, South Africa.

Apart from her educational accolades, Ohotu, as she is fondly called by friends and family, she is the founder of the society ColoredAfrica that also has a blog with the same name, which speciliazes in talking of issues that affect us as blacks, especially black women. She has a number of articles on linked in, and has written for journals and Magazines of both Law and Human rights. She hopes to make a significant difference in the way women are treated in Nigeria as a start and Africa as a whole.

Eschewing all accolades and achievements, Ohotu remains a strong woman, she is caring, generous, intelligent, understanding, and tries to be a good person with each passing day. She is God-fearing and religious, and lives by a code to do good and be good. she truly is a woman worthy of admiration and should be a role model for those who wish to fight for equity and the rights of others.
